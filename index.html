Legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

There are many legal guidelines which we must follow when starting a digital project. Copywriting GDPR - email & sms marketing - opt out procedures Website designs must carry your companies registered address & company number Using Cookies - as cookies capture data & information which is then sent back to the business, the business must therefore infom the consumer that cookies are being used. Information on public cloud systems- data protection act Computer Fraud & Abuse act Children's online privacy act

Within GDPR there are other laws or rules that must we must adhere to including the following:

  • The right to be informed is about the obligations and transparency of data being used and held to be notified and the ability to access it at a given request.
  • The right of access tells the consumer whether any data is being used with a company or any other
  • The right to rectification gives any individual the right to have any inaccurate information or data rectified.
  • The right to erasure gives individuals the right to be forgotten or for their information/data to be erased. although this right is a good thing it is not absolute and depends on the circumstances.
  • The right to restrict processing gives an individual the right to have their information/data by organisations restricted, and is an alternative to rights to erasure.
  • The right to data portability allows the data subject to request copies of all personal data which the dat subject has to provide. The right to data portability allows citizens to be able to move between various service providers without any loss of data, ie not having to put in their information again.
  • The right to object gives the individuals the right to object that their personal data not be processed and have the total rights for their data not to be used in direct marketing.
  • Rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling gives you the option to not be automatically selected for other websites, giving you the opportunity to select the ones that you do want your data shared with.

In modern day businesses GDPR is a key factor which must be considered especially when a business model relies on personal data being collected.
